# Product Design Requirements

## Product

McCaigs.com is the public and operational web platform for McCaigs, positioned as Scotland's Elite Technical Studio.

The platform must communicate capability, taste, technical depth, and trust. It should make McCaigs feel selective, hands-on, and quietly confident.

## Problem

Most AI consultancies look and sound the same. They promise transformation, automation, and innovation without showing clear engineering discipline or practical outcomes.

Scottish SMEs, startups, owner-led businesses, and specialist firms need a technical partner who can improve real operations without the usual AI hype or enterprise theatre.

## Primary audience

- Founders who need technical execution without hiring a full team.
- SMEs who need operational systems, workflow automation, and better digital products.
- Owner-led businesses with manual admin, disconnected tools, or a website that should work harder.
- Specialist service firms, local businesses, manufacturers, tourism and hospitality teams, recruitment companies, and trades.
- Scottish organisations who want a local expert partner with serious technical capability.

## Positioning statement

McCaigs is Scotland's Elite Technical Studio for ambitious businesses that need practical AI, automation, websites, internal systems, and digital products built properly.

## Goals

- Present McCaigs as premium, selective, and technically excellent.
- Move away from generic AI consultancy language.
- Explain the studio model clearly.
- Create strong enquiry paths for qualified leads.
- Support future case studies, product pages, and client portal features.
- Make practical, controlled AI a clear differentiator without overusing technical language.

## Non-goals

- Do not position McCaigs as a chatbot company.
- Do not overuse AI clichés.
- Do not make the site feel like a corporate consultancy template.
- Do not lead with tools, models, or vendor names.
- Do not promise fully autonomous outcomes without validation.

## Core pages

- Home
- Work / Builds
- Services
- Assistant
- Studio Method
- Insights
- About
- Contact
- Client Portal

## Key user journeys

### Qualified lead

Landing page → Understands positioning → Reviews services or method → Reads selected work → Submits enquiry.

### Founder or SME owner

Landing page → Identifies a practical business problem → Understands available build types → Books a discovery call.

### Returning client

Login → Client portal → Project status → Documents → Actions → Messages.

## Feature requirements

### Public website

- Strong hero section with studio positioning.
- Clear explanation of what McCaigs builds.
- Service cards grouped by outcome, not buzzword.
- Selected builds using stealth or codenamed projects where needed.
- Technical method section explaining practical, controlled delivery.
- Contact form with qualification questions.
- Blog or insights section backed by repository-managed content.

### Client portal

- Clerk authentication.
- Convex-backed organisations and client projects.
- Project timeline.
- Files and briefs.
- Action items.
- Notes and decisions.
- Status updates.

### AI layer

- AI can support summaries, brief refinement, content drafting, project notes, and internal analysis.
- AI must not own project truth.
- Every AI-generated output should be labelled, reviewable, and auditable.

## Success metrics

- More qualified enquiries.
- Clearer differentiation from generic AI agencies.
- Faster conversion from conversation to project brief.
- Reduced manual admin for project updates.
- Higher reuse of internal studio components.

## First release scope

The first release intentionally stops at a polished homepage, project enquiry flow skeleton, protected application shell, and typed backend foundation. Portal project features, content publishing, notifications, scoring, and AI assistance remain subsequent phases.
