# Design Direction

## Brand idea

McCaigs should feel like an elite technical studio hidden in plain sight. Calm, precise, premium, capable, and approachable to an ambitious business owner.

The design should suggest engineering depth without turning into a developer dashboard or an enterprise consultancy. It should feel like a creative technical studio that builds serious systems for ordinary businesses, not a SaaS template with AI gradients slapped on top.

## Visual tone

- Dark blue foundation.
- Light blue accents.
- Off-white content areas.
- Premium contrast.
- Editorial spacing.
- Mission-control details used sparingly.
- Subtle motion.
- No neon AI cliché overload.

## Colour direction

Use brand colours as system tokens rather than one-off values.

```css
--colour-midnight: #071426;
--colour-deep-blue: #0b2342;
--colour-signal-blue: #3aa7ff;
--colour-ice-blue: #d8efff;
--colour-off-white: #f6f3ec;
--colour-muted: #9fb0c3;
--colour-line: rgba(216, 239, 255, 0.16);
```

## Typography

Use typography that feels precise and modern.

Recommended direction:

- Large confident headings.
- Compact technical labels.
- Generous body copy line-height.
- Strong hierarchy.
- Avoid over-designed typography tricks.

## Website structure

The public website is multi-page. The homepage is a concise front door, not a long-form landing page.

- `/` - hero, short positioning, capability preview, selected systems preview, contact CTA.
- `/studio` - studio model, audience fit, and engineering principles.
- `/systems` - codenamed builds and system categories.
- `/services` - service categories, fit, and useful outcomes.
- `/assistant` - flagship deterministic assistant demonstration, comparison, and use cases.
- `/start-project` - structured qualification, deterministic planning estimate, and qualified enquiry handoff.
- `/insights` - practical studio notes and repository-managed publishing foundation.
- `/process` - expanded senior-led studio process.
- `/contact` - gated qualified enquiry form with a clear route to the Assistant demonstration.

## Homepage structure

### Hero

Headline:

Scotland's Elite Technical Studio

Supporting line:

Practical AI, automation, websites, internal systems, and digital products built properly for ambitious businesses.

Primary CTA:

Start a project

Secondary CTA:

View systems

### Studio proof strip

Small trust signals, not loud badges.

Examples:

- Practical AI and automation
- Direct senior involvement
- Built in Scotland
- Made for real operations

### What we build

Group by outcome:

- AI and business automation
- Internal business systems
- Websites and digital platforms
- Controlled AI assistants
- Product and SaaS builds
- Technical reviews and AI audits

### Typical problems we solve

Use recognisable operational problems: scattered enquiries, repeated admin, knowledge trapped in people's heads, underperforming websites, software workarounds, and unclear AI opportunities.

On the homepage, place these plain-English recognition cards directly before the services teaser. On the Services page, lead with a fuller common-problems section before presenting categories of work. Explain the useful business outcome for each problem before describing the technical response.

### Studio operating environment

Place the technology ecosystem directly below the hero. Present OpenAI, Anthropic, Google AI, Convex, Clerk, Vercel, Next.js, and TypeScript as integrated workshop components inside a mission-control interface. Keep the marks monochrome and palette-adapted. Never imply endorsement, partnership, or a generic trusted-by strip.

Follow the ecosystem with a practical intelligence comparison. Make the logic-first position explicit: do not pay for intelligence when logic is enough.

### Selected builds

Use codenames where commercial sensitivity matters.

Example formats:

- Project Harbour - rule-based matching engine.
- Project Ledger - operational dashboard for data-heavy workflows.
- Project Atlas - AI-assisted research and content system.

On the homepage, present these as classified studio notes with problem, solution, outcome, and build time rather than a traditional blog preview.

### Contact

Use a concise CTA into the dedicated contact page.

### Deterministic assistant

Treat the controlled assistant as a flagship studio capability rather than a small contact-page widget. The `/assistant` route opens with a guided consultation: seeded approved response, visitor questions, structured assistant blocks, suggested follow-ups, contextual actions, and a sticky input. Keep the experience calm and conversational without generic chatbot branding.

The presentation may stage approved replies briefly to make the interaction readable. It must not imply open-ended generation.

### Start a Project

Keep qualification separate from the Assistant demonstration. `/start-project` should feel like a senior discovery conversation: business details, operational problem, desired outcome, deterministic classification, weighted complexity questions, an indicative planning output, and a structured handoff. Keep the builder card dark, but place the main workflow on the same warm off-white editorial stage as the Assistant.

### Insights

Use `/insights` as the publishing home for practical studio notes, build logs, workflow guidance, internal systems thinking, and careful explanations of where AI is useful. Keep the tone commercially grounded and avoid clickbait.

## Component style

- Rounded but not playful.
- Fine borders.
- Soft depth.
- Measured animation.
- Content-first layouts.
- Mobile-first navigation.
- Avoid cramped hero sections.

## Animation rules

Use motion to guide attention, not to impress other designers on Twitter.

Allowed:

- Gentle fades.
- Slow hover transitions.
- Subtle card lift.
- Soft line reveals.
- Background grain or grid movement at very low intensity.

Avoid:

- Spinning blobs.
- Flashing gradients.
- Overanimated scroll effects.
- AI orb clichés.

## Mobile design

The mobile menu must be clean, readable, and properly branded.

- Full-screen or near full-screen overlay.
- Large touch targets.
- Clear primary CTA.
- Contact link always visible.
- No tiny hamburger-only navigation without context.

## Content tone

- Confident but understated.
- Technical but readable.
- Creative but commercially grounded.
- Premium but approachable.
- Scottish without becoming gimmicky.
- No hype language.
- No fake enterprise theatre.

## Implemented foundation

The public website uses the dark blue system, light blue signal colour, off-white editorial surfaces, restrained grid detail, codenamed system cards, and mobile sheet navigation. The dedicated Assistant page presents the controlled local demonstration, while Contact owns the gated enquiry flow.

Preferred words:

- systems
- workflows
- builds
- technical studio
- deterministic
- operational
- practical
- precise
- engineered

Avoid:

- revolutionise
- unlock your potential
- AI-powered everything
- cutting-edge for the sake of it
